Grid (graphic design)

In graphic design, a grid is a
structure (usually
two-
dimensional)
made up of a series
of
intersecting
straight
(vertical,
horizontal, and
angular) or
curved guide lines
used to
structure
content. The
grid
serves as an armature
on which
a designer
can organize
graphic
elements (
images, glyphs,
paragraphs) in a rational, easy to
absorb
manner.
Steven BradleyVanseodesign
